The Beautiful Lie

We have built our legacies on the assumption that these mountains are static. That assumption is a lie. The mountains are moving. The only shield is the forest.

The September Warnings

20,000 Cubic Meters

On September 12th, 20,000 cubic meters of rock broke off at St-Annafirn, near the ski area.[1]

Vital Infrastructure Hit

Days later, the vital Gotthard road was bombarded. These are not isolated incidents; they are warnings that the threat is accelerating.

The Escalation Scenario

The permafrost—the glue that has held the Alps together for 3,000 years—is thawing. Natural hazards cost Switzerland an average of CHF 349 million per year, a direct threat to our assets and infrastructure.[2]

Slide 2: Speaker Notes

This opening is designed to create immediate intrigue and a sense of unease, setting the stage for the urgency of the topic. The goal is to shift the audience's perception from seeing the valley as a stable, permanent fixture to recognizing its inherent volatility and the risks that lie beneath the beauty.

Slide 3: Speaker Notes

This past September, we received two sharp reminders that the mountains are not static. The St-Annafirn event highlights the importance of operational excellence; it was the vigilance and proactive risk management of the Andermatt-Sedrun Sport AG staff on the ground that ensured the area remained safe.

Slide 4: Speaker Notes

These local events are minor compared to the catastrophe in Blatten this past May. The cost of inaction is measured in lives and livelihoods, with natural hazards costing Switzerland an average of CHF 349 million per year—a figure that represents a direct threat to the assets and infrastructure we all manage.

The Naked Valley: Urserntal

Why are these warnings so critical here? Because the Ursern Valley is naked. We have only 6% forest cover—less than a third of the regional average.[3]

Next →

The 6% Lie

Even that 6% is misleading. The vast majority is weak Gebüschwald (shrubbery), useless against rockfall. Only 170 hectares of true, protective Hochwald (high forest) shield this entire valley.

Next →

The Silent Threat: Green Alder

We can't just wait for nature to fix this. Invasive Green Alder has expanded by 25% in the last decade, choking out protective conifers and reducing hydropower output by up to 11 GWh per year—a million francs in lost revenue.[4][5]

Living Infrastructure

The Barrier

Trees physically stop rocks and stabilize the snowpack.

The Anchor

100 km of roots per cubic meter pin the mountain together.[6]

The Sponge

Each m² of forest soil absorbs 200 liters of water.

High-Altitude Engineering

This isn't gardening. It takes 50-100 years for a seedling to become a fully effective protection forest. We commit to a decade of intensive care, installing tripods and protecting the 4,400 trees we've planted since 2022.[7]

The 50-Year Commitment

Our work has begun, but for critical areas like Bäz and Sattelegg, there is no long-term cantonal funding. The Verein must cover maintenance costs not for 10 years, but for up to 50. This demands sustained, serious investment from partners with a long-term view of their legacy.
