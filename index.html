"Good morning. We look at this valley and see permanence. Stability. We have built our businesses and our legacies on the assumption that these mountains are static. That assumption is a beautiful lie. The mountains are moving. The only shield is the forest."

(This opening creates intrigue and urgency.)

Slide 3: Speaker Notes

This past September, two sharp reminders: on Sept 12, ~20,000 m³ broke off at St-Annafirn. Days later, rockfall hit the Schöllenen/Gotthard corridor. These are warnings that the threat is accelerating. Note the vigilance and proactive risk management by ASSAG staff that kept people safe. (Ref. [1])

Slide 4: Speaker Notes

Contextualize with Blatten (May 2025). Permafrost—the glue of the Alps—is thawing. The cost of inaction is measured in lives and livelihoods. Natural hazards cost Switzerland ~CHF 349M per year on average. This is a direct threat to assets and infrastructure. (Ref. [2])

Slide 5: Speaker Notes

Why it matters here: Ursern is exposed. Only ~6% forest cover vs. ~20% regional average. Historically forested up to ~2,000 m, the valley was largely cleared by the 11th century—leaving today’s homes and infrastructure vulnerable. (Ref. [3])

Slide 6: Speaker Notes

The 6% is misleading. Most remaining vegetation is shrub (Gebüschwald) and provides little protection against rockfall or avalanches. Only ~170 ha of true protective high forest (Hochwald) shield the entire valley. For every one hectare protected, many lie exposed. (Ref. [3])

Slide 7: Speaker Notes

Green Alder expansion (~25% in a decade) blocks conifer regeneration and pushes toward a monoculture by ~2045 if unchecked—undermining Schutzwald function. It also reduces hydropower output by up to ~11 GWh/year (~CHF 1M revenue). This is ecological and economic. (Refs. [4], [5])

Slide 8: Speaker Notes

Invest in living infrastructure: barrier (halts rocks, stabilizes snow), anchor (up to ~100 km roots per m³), sponge (~200 L water per m²). Where obligation ends, we begin—a voluntary reforestation program to rebuild the shield where public policy falls short. (Refs. [6], [3])

Slide 9: Speaker Notes

Multi-generational engineering: protection forests take 50–100 years to fully function. We commit to 10 years of intensive care—Dreibeinböcke, browsing/erosion protection—and have planted ~4,400 trees since 2022. Community research (UZH) shows strong willingness to fund nature-based mitigation. (Refs. [7], [8])

Slide 10: Speaker Notes

Partner roles: Andermatt Swiss Alps—foundational financing; integrate forest health in development; more local wood use. ASSAG/Vail—beyond funding: mountain logistics, conservation practice (e.g., Wildruhezonen) and safe operations. Stewardship and prosperity are synergistic.

Slide 11: Speaker Notes

Concrete avalanche works ~CHF 1M/ha. Protection forests: Switzerland invests ~€145M/year; avoided damages ~€3.8B—benefit-cost > 25:1. This isn’t a donation; it’s the smartest infrastructure investment and a de-risking strategy for the entire valley. (Refs. [9], [10])

Slide 12: Speaker Notes

Critical areas (Bäz, Sattelegg) lack long-term cantonal funding. The Verein must cover not just Years 1–10 but up to 50. We need partners with a long-term view of legacy to secure the maintenance gap.

Slide 13: Speaker Notes

The seedlings we plant today will protect our grandchildren’s homes tomorrow. The mountains are moving. The only shield is the forest. Join us—funding, volunteering, CO₂ compensation—so your legacy is written in the trees that protect this valley.

The Silent Threat: Green Alder

We can’t just wait for nature to fix this. Invasive Green Alder has expanded by ~25% in the last decade, choking out protective conifers and reducing hydropower output by up to ~11 GWh/year—around a million francs in lost revenue.[4][5]

Living Infrastructure

The Barrier

Trees physically stop rocks and stabilize the snowpack.

The Anchor

100 km of roots per cubic meter pin the mountain together.[6]

The Sponge

Each m² of forest soil absorbs ~200 liters of water.[6]

Where obligation ends, we begin: a voluntary, long-horizon reforestation effort to rebuild the shield where public policy falls short.[3]

Next →

High-Altitude Engineering

This isn’t gardening. It takes 50–100 years for a seedling to become a fully effective protection forest. We commit to a decade of intensive care, installing tripods and protecting the ~4,400 trees planted since 2022. Community research shows strong support for natural reforestation alongside technical measures.[7][8]
